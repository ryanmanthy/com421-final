How to File an Appeal in the City of Chicago

Making Sense of the Jargon

Contesting a ticket can be frustrating and confusing for people. This page is designed to provide a straightforward, step-by-step process that can support you throughout your ticket appeal.

Why You Can Fight the Ticket

In Ward 44, 60% of drivers win the appeals for their parking tickets! this means that if you appeal your ticket, you have a good chance of winning. Plus, filing an appeal is free, so there is no risk in trying.

Driver, Understand Your Rights

Oftentimes people don't understand their rights when it comes to Fighting a ticket. This page will help you understand your rights and how to use them through the appeals process.

You have 7 days from the issuance of a parking violation and 21 days form an electronically (e.g red light camera) issued violation to submit an appeal.

Follow these steps to file an appeal for a ticket in the city of Chicago:

Step 1: Review the Violation Notice

Review the violation notice you received carefully. Make sure you understand what the violation is, when it occurred, and where it occurred. If you have any questions or concerns, contact the city of Chicago's Department of Finance at (312) 744-7275.

Step 2: Gather Evidence

Gather any evidence you have that supports your case. Evidence may include:

  1. Photos or videos of the scene where the violation occurred
  2. State vehicle registration
  3. Any relevant documents or records, such as official police theft or recovery reports

You should submit copies and keep the original documents for your personal records.

For a list of common defenses, please reference the our common defense strategies.

Step 3: Submit an Appeal

You can submit an appeal for your ticket in-person, online, or by mail.

Appeal Online

To appeal online, follow these steps:

  1. Visit the City of Chicago's ticket search website.
  2. Enter your ten digit ticket number.
  3. Follow the prompts to submit your appeal and any evidence you have.
Appeal by Mail

To appeal by mail, follow these steps:

  1. Complete the facts set forth and any evidence provided with your violation notice.
  2. Include any evidence you have that supports your case.
  3. Mail the form and evidence to the address provided on the violation notice.

Make sure to submit your appeal by the deadline listed on your violation notice. Appeals submitted after the deadline will not be considered.

Step 4: Wait for a Response

Upon submitting an appeal, the City of Chicago's Finance Department will reach out with a decision. This process may take several weeks.

If appealed successfully, the City of Chicago will refund any fines or fees you paid or you won't have to pay a fine. In the event of an unsuccessful appeal you can contest in court.

Step 5: Further recourse (Optional)

To contest a ticket appeal, you are able to request a hearing with the city of Chicago's through the Coolk County Circuit Court.

To request a hearing, complete the following steps:

  1. Complete the Adjudication Hearing Request Form provided with your appeal decision. All appeals are to be submitted to the Circuit Court of Cook County.
  2. Mail or deliver the form to the address provided on the appeal decision.

Make sure to submit your hearing request by the deadline listed on your appeal decision(typically 35 days). Requests submitted after the deadline will not be considered.

At the hearing, you will have the opportunity to present your case in person to an administrative judge. This judge will listen to both perspectives on the case and make a decision. Your ticket will be dismissed if the judge rules in favor. If the judge rules against you, you will still have to pay the fine.
