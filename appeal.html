You have 7 days from the issuance of a parking violation and 21 days form an electronically (e.g red light camera) issued violation to submit an appeal.

Follow these steps to file an appeal for a ticket in the city of Chicago:

Step 1: Review the Violation Notice

Review the violation notice you received carefully. Make sure you understand what the violation is, when it occurred, and where it occurred. If you have any questions or concerns, contact the city of Chicago's Department of Finance at (312) 744-7275.

Step 2: Gather Evidence

Gather any evidence you have that supports your case. Evidence may include:

  • Photos or videos of the scene where the violation occurred
  • Witness statements or contact information
  • Any relevant documents or records, such as medical records or receipts

Be sure to keep copies of all of your evidence for your records.

For a list of common defenses, please reference the city's website.

Step 3: Submit an Appeal

You can submit an appeal for your ticket in-person, online, or by mail.

Appeal Online

To appeal online, follow these steps:

  1. Visit the City of Chicago's ticket search website.
  2. Enter your ten digit ticket number.
  3. Follow the prompts to submit your appeal and any evidence you have.
Appeal by Mail

To appeal by mail, follow these steps:

  1. Complete the facts set forth and any evidence provided with your violation notice.
  2. Include any evidence you have that supports your case.
  3. Mail the form and evidence to the address provided on the violation notice.

Make sure to submit your appeal by the deadline listed on your violation notice. Appeals submitted after the deadline will not be considered.

Step 4: Wait for a Response

Once you have submitted your appeal, you will receive a response from the city of Chicago's Department of Finance. This may take several weeks.

If your appeal is successful, your ticket will be dismissed and you will not have to pay the fine. If your appeal is unsuccessful, you will have to pay the fine.

Step 5: Attend a Hearing (Optional)

If your appeal is denied and you wish to contest the decision further, you can request a hearing with the city of Chicago's Department of Administrative Hearings.

To request a hearing, follow these steps:

  1. Complete the Adjudication Hearing Request Form provided with your appeal decision. All appeals are to be submitted to the Circuit Court of Cook County.
  2. Mail or deliver the form to the address provided on the appeal decision.

Make sure to submit your hearing request by the deadline listed on your appeal decision(typically 35 days). Requests submitted after the deadline will not be considered.

At the hearing, you will have the opportunity to present your case in person to an administrative law judge. The judge will listen to both sides of the case and make a decision. If the judge rules in your favor, your ticket will be dismissed. If the judge rules against you, you will be required to pay the fine.
