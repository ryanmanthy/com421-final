Top Parking Offenses in Ward 44

On this page, we present the top parking violations in Ward 44, accompanied by brief descriptions and valuable resources to assist you in challenging and winning your parking ticket. Furthermore, we include the success rate of appeals for each violation, enabling you to assess your chances of success in court.

Expired plates or temporary registration

What is this offense?

Expired plates or temporary registration refers to the situation when a vehicle is parked on a public road with expired or missing license plates or temporary registration.

Why is this violation common in Ward 44?

The violation is common in Ward 44 because it is a residential area that experiences heavy traffic due to the presence of Wrigley Field stadium. This creates limited parking availability, causing residents to park their vehicles on the street for longer periods, leading to an increased likelihood of receiving a parking ticket for expired plates or temporary registration.

What is the success rate for this appeal?

68% of the contested parking violations in ward 44 have successfully been dismissed.

How many people have challenged this appeal in the past?

A total of 14,217 appeals have been recorded for this particular offense. This suggests that only 11% of those who received the violation attempted to contest it.

Street cleaning

What is this offense?

Street cleaning violation occurs when a vehicle is parked on a street during the scheduled street cleaning hours. In most cities, streets are cleaned on a regular schedule, and signs are posted on the streets indicating the time and date of street cleaning. If a parked vehicle obstructs the street cleaning process, it may receive a parking ticket.

Why is this violation common in Ward 44?

This violation is common in Ward 44 because the ward has designated street cleaning days where parking is prohibited on certain streets for a period of time to allow for street cleaning. Additionally, the ward is a densely populated area with limited parking availability, which can make finding alternative parking options during street cleaning hours difficult for motorists.

What is this offense?

This violation refers to parking or standing a vehicle in an area where it is prohibited at any time. This can include areas such as fire lanes, bus stops, or areas marked with "No Parking" signs.

Additionally, the ward is home to popular attractions like Wrigley Field, which increases traffic and parking congestion in the area. What is this offense?

Residential permit parking

What is this offense?

Residential permit parking is a parking violation where a driver parks in a designated residential permit parking zone without a valid permit. These zones are usually established in residential areas where parking is limited due to high demand.

Why is this violation common in Ward 44?

This violation is common in Ward 44 because it is a residential area with limited street parking available. Many residents of the ward have obtained residential parking permits that allow them to park on certain streets during designated hours. Non-permit holders who park in these areas during restricted times may receive a ticket for violating the residential permit parking regulations. Additionally, the ward has a high population density and many multi-unit residential buildings, which further increases the demand for limited street parking.
